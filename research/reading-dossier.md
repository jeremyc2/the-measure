## Themes emerging

| Theme | Working formulation | Evidence to collect |
|---|---|---|
| Measurement changes society | The string does not only reveal lifespan; it creates social categories and new incentives. | moments where people, institutions, or families behave differently because strings exist |
| Fate vs. choice | Characters must decide whether knowledge determines action or merely informs it. | decisions to open/not open boxes; decisions after learning or refusing to learn |
| Privacy and disclosure | A string can be intimate information, family information, medical information, political information, or public identity. | who tells whom; who feels entitled to know |
| Fear as social force | The boxes trigger not only grief but sorting, suspicion, and policy pressure. | public reactions, media/government reactions, interpersonal fear |
| Love under certainty/uncertainty | Relationships become tests of whether people can love across different levels of risk or knowledge. | romantic/family/friend scenes |
| Discrimination by predicted lifespan | “Short-stringer” and “long-stringer” labels risk becoming caste-like categories. | workplace, healthcare, politics, law, public spaces |

## Open questions for Chapter 10-you

- Would I open my own box? Why or why not?
- Does the string reveal fate, or does belief in the string help create fate-like outcomes?
- Who benefits from turning private lifespan knowledge into public classification?
- Who is harmed first when the world starts sorting people by string length?
- Which characters treat the box as a gift, a curse, a test, a weapon, or a diagnosis?
